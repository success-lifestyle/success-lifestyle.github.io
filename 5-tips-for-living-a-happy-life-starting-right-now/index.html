5 Tips for Living a Happy Life Starting Right Now

I am no stranger to feeling trapped in life. It doesn't matter how old you are, how much money you have in your bank account, what marital status you have or what you do for a living, we all want to live a more purposeful life and be more successful in our lives. Of course, defining success is different for each us, but here are 5 proven ways that can make you more productive, happy and successful in life.

1. Find What You Love to Do and Do It

Imagine that you are as successful as possible in your current job. Ultimately, you will probably work extremely hard and this will take a long time.

If it's a job you hate, success may just mean filling your life with something you hate. What's the point?

Instead, focus on doing something you love. Once you've figured out what you're passionate about, you'll be motivated to keep moving. Success means fulfilling your dreams.

2. Focus on commitment, not motivation.

Just how committed are you to your goal? How important is it for you, and what are you willing to sacrifice in order to achieve it? If you find yourself fully committed, motivation will follow.

3. You both need and don’t need other people.

You need others in a sense that you need to be part of a community—there’s a reason we reflexively pity hermits. Regardless of your theory of anthropogenesis, it’s hard to deny that we are built for community, and that ‘we’ is always more than ‘me.’ However, you don’t need another person in order for your life to have meaning—this idea that Disney has shoved through our eyeballs, that there’s someone out there for all of us if we’ll just believe hard enough and never stop searching, is hokum… because of arithmetic, if nothing else. Establish your own life—then, if there’s a particular person that you can’t help but integrate, believe me, you’ll know.

4. Protect yourself from burnout.

It is easy to burn out if you are very motivated. Watch yourself for signs of tiredness and take time to rest. Your body and mind will rest when you add relaxation and fun to your weekly calendar. Do different tasks, switch between something creative and logical, something physical and quiet, work alone and with a team. Change location. Meditate or take a deep breath, close your eyes, or concentrate on one thing for five minutes.

5. Be committed.

No matter what goals you set yourself in life, you have to be committed. Through your commitment, you will continue to make the improvements necessary to improve yourself. Whether it's about starting a startup, getting a gym membership to improve your physical well-being, or attending a cooking class because you want to be a chef, engagement is what drives us to become more successful.
